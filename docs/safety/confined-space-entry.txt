PROCEDURE: Confined Space Entry
CATEGORY: Safety - Wind Turbine Tower / Foundation

APPLIES TO: Turbine tower base sections, foundation chambers, and any enclosed space with limited entry/exit and potential for hazardous atmosphere.

PRE-ENTRY REQUIREMENTS:
1. Confirm a valid confined space entry permit has been issued for the specific space and date.
2. Test the atmosphere with a calibrated multi-gas monitor for oxygen level (must be 19.5%-23.5%), flammable gas, and toxic gas (e.g., CO) before entry.
3. Ensure continuous ventilation is running if required by the permit.
4. Identify and brief an attendant who will remain outside the space at all times during entry, maintaining communication with the entrant.
5. Confirm rescue equipment (retrieval line, tripod if applicable) is in place and rescue personnel/procedure is understood before entry begins.

DURING ENTRY:
1. The attendant must maintain continuous communication with the entrant (visual, voice, or radio).
2. Atmospheric monitoring should continue throughout the entry if the space or work presents a risk of changing conditions.
3. If atmospheric alarms trigger, or communication with the entrant is lost, the entrant must exit immediately and the attendant must never enter the space to attempt an unassisted rescue — follow the site's rescue procedure instead.

POST-ENTRY:
1. Confirm all personnel and tools are out of the space.
2. Close out the entry permit and log the entry.

SAFETY NOTES:
- Never enter a confined space without a valid permit and a dedicated attendant, even for a "quick look."
- Foundation chambers and tower base sections can accumulate low-oxygen or hazardous atmospheres even without an obvious source — always test before entry, regardless of prior experience with the space.
