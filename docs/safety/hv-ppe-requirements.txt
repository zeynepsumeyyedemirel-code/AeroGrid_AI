PROCEDURE: High-Voltage PPE Requirements
CATEGORY: Safety - Personal Protective Equipment

PURPOSE:
To define the minimum PPE required when working on or near high-voltage components in wind turbine generators, solar inverters, and combiner/switchgear.

REQUIRED PPE FOR HV ELECTRICAL WORK:
- Voltage-rated insulating gloves, tested and rated for the voltage class of the equipment, with leather protector gloves worn over them.
- Arc-rated clothing appropriate to the PPE category specified on the equipment's arc flash label.
- Safety glasses or face shield, per the arc flash category.
- Insulated, voltage-rated hand tools when any contact with conductors is possible.
- Non-conductive, electrical-hazard-rated footwear.
- Hard hat, for work in nacelles, combiner rooms, or near overhead equipment.

INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS:
1. Insulating gloves must be visually inspected and air-tested before each use for punctures or degradation.
2. Insulating gloves must be electrically re-tested at the interval specified by the manufacturer/site policy (commonly every 6 months) — check the test date stamp before use.
3. Arc-rated clothing must be free of contamination (oil, flammable residue) and free of damage (tears, excessive wear) before use.
4. Any PPE that fails inspection must be removed from service immediately and replaced.

GENERAL RULES:
- PPE is a last line of defense — always follow LOTO isolation and verify zero energy before relying on PPE alone.
- Do not modify or repair damaged arc-rated PPE in the field; retire and replace it.
- Cotton or synthetic clothing that can melt is not a substitute for arc-rated clothing in HV work areas.

SAFETY NOTES:
- Match PPE category to the specific equipment's arc flash label — do not assume the same PPE level is sufficient across all equipment on site.
