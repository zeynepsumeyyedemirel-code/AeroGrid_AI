PROCEDURE: Fall Protection for Work at Height
CATEGORY: Safety - Wind Turbine Tower, Nacelle & Rooftop Solar

APPLIES TO: Turbine tower climbing, nacelle roof access, blade/hub access, and rooftop solar installations.

REQUIREMENTS BEFORE WORK AT HEIGHT:
1. Confirm the fall protection harness and lanyard/self-retracting lifeline are within their inspection date and show no visible damage (frayed webbing, damaged hardware, corrosion).
2. Confirm 100% tie-off practice: at least one anchor point connection must be maintained at all times when transitioning between anchor points.
3. Identify the rescue plan for the specific location before beginning work — know how a suspended or incapacitated worker would be retrieved.
4. Check weather conditions; do not perform tower, nacelle, or rooftop work in high wind, lightning risk, or icy conditions per site policy thresholds.

DURING WORK:
1. Use only anchor points rated and designated for fall arrest — never anchor to unrated structural elements, railings not designed for fall arrest, or equipment components.
2. Maintain three points of contact when climbing fixed ladders inside the tower, using the ladder's integrated fall arrest system if equipped.
3. On nacelle roofs and rooftop solar arrays, be aware of edge proximity and use guardrails or continuous tie-off at all times near an unprotected edge.

SUSPENSION TRAUMA AWARENESS:
- A worker suspended in a harness after a fall is at risk of suspension trauma if not rescued promptly. Rescue should be initiated as quickly as possible, targeting well under the site's maximum suspension time policy.

SAFETY NOTES:
- Never work at height alone without a means of summoning help (radio/phone) and a known rescue plan.
- Fall protection equipment is single-use after arresting a fall — remove it from service and inspect/replace per manufacturer guidance even if no visible damage is present.
