FAULT CODE: E-101
TITLE: Pitch Control System Fault
CATEGORY: Wind Turbine - Hydraulic / Electrical

SYMPTOMS:
- Blade pitch angle deviates from the commanded setpoint by more than 2 degrees.
- SCADA alarm "PITCH-DEV" logged with the affected blade number (1, 2, or 3).
- Turbine automatically de-rates or enters safe-stop mode.

LIKELY CAUSES:
- Hydraulic accumulator pressure below the 160 bar minimum threshold.
- Pitch actuator encoder drift or contamination.
- Leaking pitch cylinder seal reducing actuation force.

DIAGNOSTIC STEPS:
1. Check hydraulic accumulator pressure gauge on the affected blade's pitch unit; compare against the 160-210 bar operating range.
2. Inspect the pitch encoder cable connections for corrosion or looseness.
3. Cross-reference the SCADA pitch angle log for the last 24 hours to identify gradual drift vs. sudden fault.
4. Visually inspect the pitch cylinder for hydraulic fluid leakage at the rod seal.

RESOLUTION:
1. If accumulator pressure is low, recharge using the nitrogen charging kit to the value listed on the accumulator nameplate.
2. If the encoder is contaminated, clean per the manufacturer's encoder maintenance procedure and re-zero the pitch reference.
3. If a seal leak is confirmed, tag the turbine out of service and schedule a cylinder seal replacement; do not attempt to run the turbine with active hydraulic leakage.
4. After any repair, run the manual pitch test sequence from the nacelle control panel before returning the turbine to automatic operation.

SAFETY NOTES:
- Hydraulic systems on this unit operate up to 210 bar. Always relieve pressure before disconnecting any hydraulic line.
- Do not place hands or tools within the pitch bearing sweep radius while the rotor is unlocked.
- Confirm rotor lock is engaged before entering the hub.
