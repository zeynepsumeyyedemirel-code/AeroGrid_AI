FAULT CODE: E-150
TITLE: Anemometer / Wind Vane Fault
CATEGORY: Wind Turbine - Sensors / Control

SYMPTOMS:
- SCADA reports a flatlined or erratic wind speed reading (e.g., stuck at 0 m/s while other turbines in the array show normal wind).
- Turbine fails to start automatically despite adequate site wind conditions.
- Alarm "ANEMO-FLT" or "WINDVANE-FLT".

LIKELY CAUSES:
- Ice accumulation on the anemometer cups or wind vane (common in winter conditions).
- Failed anemometer bearing causing the cup assembly to stop spinning freely.
- Damaged signal cable between the nacelle roof sensor and the control cabinet.

DIAGNOSTIC STEPS:
1. Compare this turbine's wind speed reading against neighboring turbines in the same array for the same time window.
2. Visually inspect the anemometer and wind vane on the nacelle roof (from ground level with binoculars, or via roof access if safe) for ice or physical damage.
3. Check the sensor signal cable continuity at the nacelle control cabinet terminal block.
4. If the turbine has a redundant (secondary) anemometer, compare readings between primary and secondary sensors.

RESOLUTION:
1. If ice is present, this is often a self-clearing seasonal condition; confirm the turbine's ice-detection logic is functioning and not being bypassed.
2. If the anemometer bearing has failed, replace the anemometer unit; do not attempt to lubricate a sealed bearing assembly in the field.
3. If cable continuity fails, trace and repair or replace the damaged section of cable.
4. After sensor replacement, recalibrate against a handheld reference anemometer before returning to automatic control.

SAFETY NOTES:
- Nacelle roof access requires fall protection and a rescue plan per site policy; do not access the roof alone.
- Do not attempt roof access in high wind or icy conditions — schedule the visit for a calm-weather window.
