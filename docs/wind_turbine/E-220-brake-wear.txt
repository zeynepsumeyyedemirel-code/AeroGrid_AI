FAULT CODE: E-220
TITLE: Mechanical Brake Pad Wear Limit
CATEGORY: Wind Turbine - Mechanical / Braking System

SYMPTOMS:
- SCADA alarm "BRAKE-WEAR" triggered by the brake pad wear sensor.
- Extended stopping distance/time observed during scheduled brake tests.
- Brake caliper temperature reading higher than normal after a stop event.

LIKELY CAUSES:
- Brake pads worn below the minimum thickness threshold due to normal service life.
- Frequent emergency stop events accelerating pad wear.
- Brake caliper misalignment causing uneven pad wear.

DIAGNOSTIC STEPS:
1. Read the wear sensor value from the SCADA maintenance screen and compare to the minimum thickness spec.
2. Physically inspect brake pad thickness with calipers if the sensor reading is inconclusive.
3. Check the brake disc surface for scoring or uneven wear pattern, which may indicate caliper misalignment.
4. Review the SCADA event log for the number of full/emergency stops in the last maintenance interval.

RESOLUTION:
1. Replace brake pads as a set (not individually) once below the minimum thickness spec.
2. Re-torque caliper mounting bolts to the manufacturer's specified value after pad replacement.
3. If disc scoring is present, measure disc thickness against the minimum spec; resurface or replace if out of tolerance.
4. Perform a full brake function test (normal stop and emergency stop) before returning the turbine to service.

SAFETY NOTES:
- Ensure the rotor is locked and the turbine is isolated from automatic control before working on the braking system.
- Brake discs and calipers may be hot after recent operation; allow cooling time before contact.
