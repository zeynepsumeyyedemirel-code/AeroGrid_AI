FAULT CODE: E-501
TITLE: Gearbox Excessive Vibration
CATEGORY: Wind Turbine - Drivetrain

SYMPTOMS:
- Vibration monitoring system (CMS) reports RMS vibration above the alarm threshold on the gearbox intermediate or high-speed shaft.
- Audible whining or knocking noise from the drivetrain during operation.
- SCADA alarm "GBX-VIB".

LIKELY CAUSES:
- Bearing wear or early-stage bearing failure within the gearbox.
- Gear tooth surface damage (pitting or spalling).
- Low or contaminated gearbox oil level.

DIAGNOSTIC STEPS:
1. Review the CMS vibration spectrum for characteristic bearing defect frequencies versus gear-mesh frequencies to help localize the fault.
2. Check gearbox oil level and condition; take an oil sample for particle analysis if contamination is suspected.
3. Inspect the oil filter and magnetic chip detector for metal debris, which indicates active wear.
4. Cross-check oil temperature trend; elevated temperature combined with vibration often confirms a bearing issue.

RESOLUTION:
1. If chip detector debris is minor and vibration is below the critical threshold, schedule increased monitoring and a borescope inspection at the next opportunity.
2. If vibration is above the critical threshold or significant metal debris is found, de-rate or stop the turbine and schedule a full gearbox inspection/overhaul — continued operation risks catastrophic gearbox failure.
3. Top up or replace gearbox oil per the maintenance schedule; always use the manufacturer-specified oil grade.
4. Document all CMS readings and oil analysis results in the maintenance log for trend tracking.

SAFETY NOTES:
- Do not open gearbox inspection covers while the drivetrain is rotating or capable of rotating; confirm rotor lock and full LOTO isolation first.
- Hot gearbox oil can cause burns; allow the unit to cool before sampling.
- Use proper PPE (gloves, eye protection) when handling gearbox oil samples.
