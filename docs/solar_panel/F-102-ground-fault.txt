FAULT CODE: F-102
TITLE: Ground Fault Detected
CATEGORY: Solar PV - DC Array / Safety

SYMPTOMS:
- Inverter or combiner box ground fault indicator (GFDI) is illuminated/tripped.
- Alarm "GND-FLT" logged with the affected string or combiner box number.
- Array production drops for the affected string(s) while others remain normal.

LIKELY CAUSES:
- Damaged module or connector insulation allowing a conductor to contact a grounded surface.
- Water ingress into a junction box or connector.
- Rodent damage to DC cabling.

DIAGNOSTIC STEPS:
1. Identify the affected string/combiner box from the fault log.
2. With the array de-energized and isolated (see LOTO procedure), perform an insulation resistance (megger) test on the affected string, per the site's testing procedure.
3. Visually inspect module junction boxes and connectors along the affected string for water ingress, cracked housings, or rodent damage.
4. Check MC4 connector mating for corrosion or incomplete engagement.

RESOLUTION:
1. Repair or replace any damaged module, connector, or cable segment identified as the fault source.
2. Reseal junction boxes where water ingress is found; replace the junction box if internal corrosion is present.
3. Re-test insulation resistance after repair; value must meet the minimum spec (typically ≥1 MΩ) before re-energizing.
4. Reset the ground fault indicator only after the fault source is confirmed corrected — do not reset repeatedly without diagnosis, as this can mask a developing hazard.

SAFETY NOTES:
- A ground fault indicates a potential shock hazard. Do not touch array wiring, even with gloves, until the affected string is confirmed de-energized and isolated.
- Follow full LOTO isolation and use a rated DC voltage meter to confirm zero energy before beginning work.
- Wet conditions increase shock risk; postpone hands-on diagnosis during active rain if possible.
