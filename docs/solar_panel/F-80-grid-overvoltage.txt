FAULT CODE: F-80
TITLE: Grid Overvoltage Trip
CATEGORY: Solar PV - Inverter / Grid Interconnection

SYMPTOMS:
- Inverter trips offline with alarm "GRID-OV" (grid overvoltage).
- AC output voltage reading exceeds the utility interconnection limit (typically 110% of nominal, confirm against the local interconnection agreement).
- Repeated trip/reconnect cycling during high-irradiance, low-load periods.

LIKELY CAUSES:
- High local grid voltage caused by excess distributed generation on the feeder (common at midday peak solar production).
- Undersized AC cabling causing voltage rise between the inverter and the point of common coupling.
- Inverter voltage ride-through / reactive power settings not configured per the local grid code.

DIAGNOSTIC STEPS:
1. Check the inverter event log for the exact voltage value and timestamp at trip.
2. Measure AC voltage directly at the point of common coupling versus at the inverter terminals to check for excessive voltage rise across the cabling.
3. Confirm the inverter's volt-var / voltage ride-through settings match the utility's current interconnection requirements.
4. Check whether the trip correlates with peak production hours (11am-2pm) or with utility-side switching events.

RESOLUTION:
1. If voltage rise across cabling is excessive, this indicates undersized conductors; consult the site's electrical design for a cable upgrade.
2. Reconfigure inverter reactive power / volt-var settings per the utility's latest interconnection requirements, if authorized to do so.
3. If the issue is grid-side (feeder voltage itself is high), report to the utility/grid operator — this is outside the scope of on-site correction.
4. Confirm normal reconnection after voltage returns to acceptable range and the inverter's reconnect delay timer has elapsed.

SAFETY NOTES:
- Do not bypass or disable inverter protective trip settings; overvoltage protection exists to protect both equipment and grid stability.
- Use a properly rated meter for AC voltage measurements at the point of common coupling.
