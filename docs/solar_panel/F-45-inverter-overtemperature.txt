FAULT CODE: F-45
TITLE: Inverter Over-Temperature
CATEGORY: Solar PV - Inverter

SYMPTOMS:
- Inverter derates output power or shuts down with alarm "INV-OT".
- Internal heatsink temperature sensor reads above the manufacturer's threshold (typically 75-85°C depending on model).
- Cooling fan runs continuously at high speed without bringing temperature down.

LIKELY CAUSES:
- Blocked or dirty inverter cooling fins/heatsink.
- Failed or degraded cooling fan.
- Inverter installed in a location with inadequate ventilation or direct sun exposure exceeding the ambient temperature rating.
- Sustained operation near the inverter's maximum power rating during high-irradiance conditions.

DIAGNOSTIC STEPS:
1. Check the inverter's internal temperature log to see if the rise is gradual (pointing to a cooling problem) or sudden (pointing to a load or fan failure).
2. Inspect and clean the heatsink fins and air intake/exhaust vents for dust, pollen, or debris buildup.
3. Verify cooling fan operation; listen for unusual noise or check fan current draw if accessible via the diagnostic menu.
4. Confirm ambient temperature at the inverter enclosure is within the manufacturer's rated operating range.

RESOLUTION:
1. Clean the heatsink and vents; this resolves the majority of over-temperature events.
2. Replace a failed or underperforming cooling fan.
3. If ambient conditions exceed the rated range, consider adding shading or improving enclosure ventilation.
4. Allow the inverter to cool fully before restarting; repeated forced restarts on a hot unit can accelerate component wear.

SAFETY NOTES:
- Isolate the inverter (both AC and DC sides) per LOTO procedure before opening any panel for cleaning or fan inspection.
- Allow adequate cool-down time — internal components and heatsinks can remain hot enough to cause burns for 15-30 minutes after shutdown.
